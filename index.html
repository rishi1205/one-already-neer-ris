How We Met
Baku, Azerbaijan ✈
The Airport
You were calmly photographing the sunrise. I couldn't look away. I copied your exact angle — same shot, same spot — just to feel closer to you.
The Bus, Qabala
We sat on opposite sides. Not a single word — but we kept finding each other in the rear view mirror.
The Fence
You were climbing. I offered my hand. The world went quiet. After that, we couldn't stop talking.
The River · Ropeway · Rooftop
Four hours vanished. We went everywhere we weren't supposed to. Our parents had to call us back.

The Exchange
You gave me your favourite scrunchy. I gave you my bracelet — 23 beads, just like your birthday.
The Miracle
Your family accidentally booked the 2 AM flight. You stayed five extra days. I drove back. Some things are simply meant.

When It Became Official
26 May 2024  ·  9:11 PM
You were at your aunty's apartment in Ahmedabad. I came with a paper rose I had folded — nothing grand, just everything I had.

I started talking. I couldn't stop. For 14 whole minutes, I yapped — about Baku, the sunrise, the fence, the bracelet, the scrunchy, about you. I never once said like. I always knew it was love, so that's exactly what I said.

At 8:57 PM, I told you I love you.
At 9:11 PM, I asked you to be my girlfriend.
And you said yes.

Then we went on morning walks. Then badminton. I kept picking flowers off the ground and giving them to you after every game.
8:57 PM "I love you." — said like I'd always known it, because I had.
9:11 PM 14 minutes of yapping later — "Will you be my girlfriend?" She said yes.
"He came with a paper rose
and 14 minutes of everything
he'd been holding since Baku."
